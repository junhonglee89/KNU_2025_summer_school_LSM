1. Land surface model 개요#

1.1. 정의#

Land Surface Model (LSM, 지면 모수화)

The model represents interactions between land and atmosphere (Fig. 2).

  1. LSM computes energy/water/carbon fluxes & budgets between land and atmosphere.

  2. LSM computes energy/water/carbon states in land.




1.2 필요성#

  • Atmospheric model predicts atmospheric states using primitive equations.

  • To solve primitive equations, initial & boundary conditions are required.

    • Initial condition includes temperature, wind, humidity fields
      → provided as initial condition file at the beginning of integration.

  • Boundary condition includes temperature, wind, humidity fields at 1) upper boundary, 2) lower boundary.
    1) Upper boundary condition includes solar radiation from the space
         → estimated by radiation scheme based on lat-lon, year, and day of the year.
    2) Lower boundary condition includes energy/water/carbon fluxes
         → provided by land surface model/ocean model.




1.2.1 Energy budget#

Land perspective

  • Energy in land is balanced between 1) the net amount of radiation (\(R_n\)) and 2) three heat fluxes (Fig. 4, [Sellers et al., 1997]).

Net amount of radiation

\[ R_n = (1-\alpha) \cdot SW_d + LW_d - LW_u \tag{1.1} \]

where \(\alpha\) is the surface albedo, \(SW_d\) is downward solar radiation at the surface, \(LW_d\) downward longwave radiation at the surface, \(LW_u\) upward longwave radiation at the surface.

Three heat fluxes

  1. sensitble heat flux (\(H\)),

  2. latent heat flux (\(\lambda E\)),

  3. ground heat flux (\(G\)).

\[ C_{soil} \frac{\partial T_{s}}{\partial t} = R_n - H - \lambda E - G \tag{1.2} \]

where \(C_{soil}\) is the area-specific heat capacity of the uppermost soil layer [J/m2/K1]

  • Generally, if \(R_n > H + \lambda E + G\) → land surface warming.

  • The land surface:

    1. gains energy through \(SW_d\) and \(LW_d\) from the atmosphere.

    2. looses energy through \(H\), \(\lambda E\), and \(\epsilon \sigma T_s^4\) to the atmosphere.

\(\therefore\) The atmosphere acts as a upper boundary condition.



Atmosphere perspective

  • The atmosphere:

    1. looses energy through \(LW_d\) to the land surface.

    2. gains energy through \(H\), \(\lambda E\), and \(\epsilon \sigma T_s^4\) from the land surface.

\(\therefore\) The land surface acts as a lower boundary condition.




1.2.2 Water budget#

Land perspective

  • Water in land is balanced between 1) gain from the atmosphere and 2) loss to the atmosphere and ocean (Fig. 5, [Üreyen, 2022]).

  1. gains water through precipitation from the atmosphere.

  2. looses water through evapotranspiration, runoff, and drainage (groundwater flow) to the atmosphere and ocean.

\(\therefore\) The atmosphere acts as a upper boundary condition.

Atmosphere perspective

  • Water in the atmosphere is balanced betwee 1) gain from the land and 2) loss to the land and ocean

  1. looses water through precipitation to land and ocean.

  2. gain water through evapotranspiration from the land and ocean.

\(\therefore\) The land surface acts as a lower boundary condition.




1.2.3 Carbon budget#

  • Similarly, balance between carbon absorption and carbon emission/release (Fig. 5, [Friedlingstein et al., 2022]).

  • But, carbon budget will not be discussed in this lecture.
